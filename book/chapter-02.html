Chapter 02

SCRUM for lean startups

A methodology is not “advice”. It is a shared operating system.

Early-stage startups fail less from “bad ideas” and more from chaos: unclear roles, unpredictable progress, and no shared cadence. When a team is part-time and volunteer-driven, chaos is fatal.

Scrum works because it gives teams a rhythm and a language. This framework aims to do the same for lean startups: a light, repeatable system that converts motivation into shipped product.

Tagline

The Zero-Capital Startup Framework is SCRUM for lean startups — guidelines for building a team, running rituals, and shipping an MVP into production without funding.

The three layers

menu_book
Book

Methodology

apps

Tracks tasks, hiring, recognition, and the team’s operating rhythm.

What makes this different

  • check_circle Team-first: you recruit a real team early, not “later when funded”.
  • check_circle Cadence-first: you protect momentum with rituals and visible progress.
  • check_circle Production-first: “MVP in prod” beats endless prototyping.
  • check_circle Volunteer-realistic: scope is limited to what part-time teams can actually ship.
